Balancing supply and demand: Review of the 2018 donor heart allocation policy. Heart transplant remains the most effective treatment for patients with end stage heart failure. Advances in mechanical circulatory support devices have changed the therapeutic landscape and contributed to a demographic shift in patients awaiting transplant. In the setting of a growing waitlist and concern for an inability of current policies to accurately account for patient acuity and equitable geographic distribution of organs, the United Network for Organ Sharing developed a new donor heart allocation policy which was introduced in 2018. The new policy creates more precise listing criteria to reflect patient acuity, addresses previously marginalized groups, and takes steps to address geographic inequalities.
