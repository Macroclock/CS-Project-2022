[Neurotoxicity of cyclosporine]. Using cyclosporine as an immunosuppressive drug allowed increasing survival of grafts as well as patients. Despite this drug is being safely used for many years, it can cause complications. The most often complications are nefrotoxicity, hepatotoxicity, diabetes mellitus, arterial hypertension and neurotoxicity. The most often manifestation of neurotoxicity are tremor, leucoencephalopathia, headache and even seizures. Among risk factors we can enumerate arterial hypertetension, low cholesterol level, hypomagnesemia and previous cerebrovascular insufficiency.
