Inappropriate ICD Shock From Perceived Ventricular Fibrillation During Balloon Manipulation at the Time of Percutaneous Coronary Intervention. The authors demonstrate that device manipulation during percutaneous coronary intervention can result in "noise," which can be perceived as an arrhythmia resulting in an inappropriate shock. Although rare, this possibility should be considered when an operator encounters a difficult to traverse lesion in a patient with an ICD.
