Early-onset posterior reversible encephalopathy syndrome after solid organ transplantation in pediatric patients: a report of 2 cases. Posterior reversible encephalopathy syndrome (PRES) is a neurological disturbance that occurs due to different reasons and presents with different clinical symptoms. It can be a devastating situation, but, timely treatment may lead to complete recovery. We report 2 cases of PRES, which developed and fully recovered in the early period after solid organ transplantation in pediatric patients.
