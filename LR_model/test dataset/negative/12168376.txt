Reducing the shortage of donor hearts in the UK. Improvements in the treatment of heart failure have led to a fall in the number of people requiring heart transplants in recent years. However, there is still a shortage of organs, as the number of donors has also dropped. This article looks at the process for selecting cardiac recipients and the support offered to people awaiting a transplant. It also explains how a heart transplant is coordinated and outlines plans to increase the number of hearts made available for transplant.
